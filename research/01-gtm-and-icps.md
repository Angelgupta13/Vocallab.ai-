# GTM & ICPs — Research Notes

## Homepage Content Audit

The vocallabs.ai homepage has exactly one hero value prop:
> "Conversational AI platform for modern businesses"

A single page serves all visitors. Nav has 11 items but no "Pricing", no "Integrations", no "Case Studies", no "India" page.

### ICP Mapping (who should land, who actually converts)

| BFSI compliance officer | "voice AI for collections India" | Generic homepage | Gnani.ai: "200+ enterprise clients, 12 Indian languages" |

## India-Specific Positioning Gap

### What exists but isn't surfaced
- Exotel SIP trunking integration (documented on Exotel's site)
- Indian language STT/TTS (exists in API but not listed on site)
- Bengaluru headquarters / IIT Madras founder background (not on About page)
- Government recognition (Top 5 AI Startup, Karnataka — Feb 2026)

### What competitors show for India
- **Gnani.ai**: "India's Leading Conversational AI Platform" — homepage hero, 12 languages listed, Indian case studies
- **Bolna**: "Voice AI for Indian enterprises" — homepage, 10 languages, waitlist for India
- **Bland AI**: 40+ languages including Hindi — global page but listed
- **Vapi**: No India-specific positioning — but expanding through channel partners

## Subspace Connection

Subspace.money is built for India-first consumers. The product, pricing (INR), and messaging are all India-native. Joey Dash's Subspace experience should make Vocallabs' India positioning more natural — but Vocallabs reads as a generic API platform. The contrast between the two companies:
- Subspace: "Subscribe to anything, delivered in minutes" — clear, specific, India-relevant
- Vocallabs: "Conversational AI platform for modern businesses" — generic, global, unspecific

This suggests the India positioning is intentional at the company level but not executed at the product/marketing level. Likely a resource constraint, not a strategic omission.
